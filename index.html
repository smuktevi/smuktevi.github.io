My Story

At a very young age I spent a lot of time playing online games on my dad’s old Compaq Presario in America. Later on, I had to leave and go to a boarding school in rural India which had restricted students’ access to internet, computers and TV. I was fascinated by computers, but I knew nothing of their potential till late high school. Becoming anything related to a computer or internet-related field was just a pipe dream to me. A fantasy fueled only by the sci-fi movies that I could get my hands on during the holidays.

Fast forward a few years into the future. Now I’m back in America as a student at the University of Washington, pursuing a Master’s degree in Data Science. What happened in between? Going back, here are a few highlights. I…

  • Worked at IBM as a Developer on the Cognitive Data Science team. Developed a versatile skillset ranging from automation using python scripting to working with MEAN stack development.
  • Worked at a DeepTech start-up as an Intern. Built models with Deep Belief Nets and RBMs boosting the recommender system functionalities of their flagship product.
  • Conceptualized and built a prototype web-app called “LyriQuest” which had multiple features. The main one being sentiment analysis on songs by applying NLP techniques on their lyrics. Won the best project award for this application in my senior year.
  • Collaborated on a minor thesis paper on the data analysis of road traffic accidents. It was accepted by IEEE after being presented at a conference.

The Computer Science class that I took late in high school was what kindled the fire to make all of the above possible. Before leaving school, I built a game on TurboC++. It was inspired by a game I used to play on the old Compaq, called Pipe Dream.

There are no pipe dreams.
